If you want to set up a service community for your end-to-end service, email us or read more about working across organisational boundaries.

The time it takes to set up a service community will vary depending on how complex your service is and how many departments need to be involved. For example, Get health benefits had 3 phone calls with the team at GDS in September and ran their first workshop in November. In that time, they wrote a proposal document and invited people to attend a workshop. Once your community is up and running, you will have to design and lead workshops, keep track of what the community is working on, and onboard new people.

We will help you make a formal case for collaboration and support you in scoping your service and designing workshops. We also provide guidance on how to collaborate in a sustainable way and case studies showing how others collaborated.

By setting up a service community, you will be able to:

  • contribute to government objectives
  • better understand the users of your service
  • access people and departments you need to collaborate with
  • get support from other departments to understand and improve your service
  • have a holistic view of the end to end service that your service is part of
  • reduce time and effort to connect with relevant departments
  • improve your skills in understanding both the detail of a service and the wider context in which it exists (technical, organisational, societal, political)
  • influence key decisions and shape design of services
  • increase visibility of the work you are doing
  • improve your facilitation and collaboration skills by running community workshops
  • use tools to better understand the your users of your end-to-end services

If you want to set up a service community for your end-to-end service, email us or read more about working across organisational boundaries.
